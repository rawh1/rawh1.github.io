Re-Presenting the City’s Image: Theaters in a Theater for Paris

From the mid- to late nineteenth century, Georges-Eugène Haussmann orchestrated a renovation of Paris focused on the city’s imageability. The scheme’s straight, regular boulevards produce perfect perspectival vistas whose optical effects are comparable to those achieved by the lens of a camera.

Interior Space

The project reorganizes, amplifies, and reaffiliates interior elements – such as the iconic grand stair and round rooms.

Reimagining Context

This thesis negotiates both Garnier’s interior spatial typologies and Haussmann’s exterior urban context by examining the Palais Garnier via its neglected vantage points.

Examination of Facades and Vantage Points

The project examines each of the opera house's facades overlaying line-of-sight data.

The blue coloration on this image marks portions of the building that are visible from the approaching streets; the darker the color, the greater the number of vantage points from which that portion of the facade can be clearly seen.
